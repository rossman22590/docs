# ComposeCraft: AI-Powered Music Composition

<Tip>
  Transform your lyrics into a full song by providing reference audio with ComposeCraft!
</Tip>

## Overview

ComposeCraft is an AI-driven tool that allows you to generate original music based on your provided lyrics and a reference audio track. By combining your lyrical ideas with the style or structure of existing audio, ComposeCraft helps you bring your musical vision to life. It's a straightforward process designed for lyricists, musicians, and anyone looking to create unique music with AI assistance.

## How to Use ComposeCraft

Follow these steps to compose music with ComposeCraft:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter Your Lyrics" icon="keyboard">
    Input your song lyrics into the **Lyrics** text area.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Provide Reference Audio" icon="upload">
    Choose how to provide reference audio:
    - **Upload Reference Audio**: Click **Choose File** to upload an audio file from your device (MP3 or WAV recommended).
    - **Reference Audio URL**: Enter the URL of an existing audio file online.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Compose Music" icon="play">
    Click the **Compose Music** button. Ensure you have enough credits (20 credits per song). The AI will generate the music based on your lyrics and the reference audio.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Input Parameters and Options

ComposeCraft requires your lyrics and a reference audio source:

<ResponseField name="Lyrics" type="string" required>
  Your song lyrics. Maximum 600 characters.
</ResponseField>
<ResponseField name="Upload Reference Audio" type="file">
  Upload an audio file from your device to use as a reference.
</ResponseField>
<ResponseField name="Reference Audio URL" type="string" required>
  Enter the URL of an audio file to use as a reference. Accepts .wav or .mp3 formats.
</ResponseField>

ComposeCraft saves your generated songs for easy access.

## Credits

Generating music with ComposeCraft costs **20 credits per song**. Your current credit balance is displayed at the top left of the interface.
