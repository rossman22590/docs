# TalkCraft Ultra: Advanced Lip-Sync Technology

<Tip>
  Transform existing videos by perfectly syncing lip movements to new audio tracks, creating seamless and realistic dubbed content!
</Tip>

## Overview

TalkCraft Ultra is a cutting-edge AI tool that allows you to resynchronize lip movements in videos to match new audio input. This powerful technology enables you to create dubbed content, update existing videos with new dialogue, or fix out-of-sync audio issues with ease.

## How to Use TalkCraft Ultra

Follow these steps to create your lip-synced video:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload Video" icon="film">
    Add a video of a person talking. 
    
    <Frame>
      <img src="https://img.mytsi.org/i/hKtR802.png" alt="Video Upload Interface" />
    </Frame>
    
    <Callout type="warning">
      Be mindful of the 32MB upload limit. If the video is shorter than the audio, it will be looped to match the audio length.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Audio" icon="music">
    Upload the audio file you want to sync with the video.
    
    <Frame>
      <img src="https://img.mytsi.org/i/CIBv803.png" alt="Audio Upload Interface" />
    </Frame>
    
    <Callout type="info">
      The length of the final video will depend on the length of the audio file. You can upload up to 2 minutes.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Model" icon="code-branch">
    Choose between stable and beta models.
    
    <Callout type="tip">
      Beta models may be less stable but potentially produce better results.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose Output Resolution" icon="expand">
    Select the desired output resolution, which will determine the aspect ratio of the video.
    
    <Frame>
      <img src="https://img.mytsi.org/i/6HaE804.png" alt="Output Resolution Selection" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Sync Lip Movements" icon="play">
    Click the "Sync Lip Movements" button to start the process.
    
    <Frame>
      <img src="https://img.mytsi.org/i/pg9h805.png" alt="Sync Process Interface" />
    </Frame>
    
    <Callout type="info">
      The process typically takes up to 5 minutes, depending on the length of the audio. You can view the status at the bottom of the screen.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Best Practices for TalkCraft Ultra

To achieve the best results with TalkCraft Ultra, consider these tips:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Choosing the Right Video" icon="video">
    - Use videos with clear, front-facing shots of the speaker
    - Ensure good lighting and minimal background movement
    - Choose videos where the speaker's mouth is clearly visible
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Preparing Your Audio" icon="headphones">
    - Use high-quality audio recordings with clear speech
    - Match the pacing of the audio to the original video for more natural results
    - Consider the emotional tone of the audio to match the video's visual expressions
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Selecting the Appropriate Model" icon="microscope">
    - Start with the stable model for consistent results
    - Experiment with beta models for potentially higher quality output
    - Compare results from different models to find the best fit for your content
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Optimizing Output Resolution" icon="gear">
    - Choose a resolution that matches your intended platform (e.g., YouTube, Instagram)
    - Consider the original video's quality when selecting output resolution
    - Test different resolutions to find the best balance between quality and file size
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

If you encounter issues with TalkCraft Ultra, try these solutions:

1. **Poor Lip-Sync**: Ensure your audio is clear and matches the pacing of the original video.
2. **Processing Errors**: Check that your video and audio files are within the size limit and in supported formats.
3. **Unnatural Movements**: Try using the stable model instead of beta, or vice versa.
4. **Low Output Quality**: Experiment with different output resolutions or use higher quality input files.
