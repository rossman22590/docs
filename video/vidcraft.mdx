# VidCraft: AI Video Generation

<Tip>
  Turn your images into captivating 5-second videos using Midjourney!
</Tip>

## Overview

VidCraft, powered by Midjourney's video model, allows you to turn a single image into a dynamic 5-second video. It's as simple as feeding VidCraft an image to serve as the first frame, adding an optional text prompt, and then watching as it brings your vision to life with a moving sequence.

## Features

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Image to Video" icon="image">
    Transform a static image into a 5-second video.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Looping Videos" icon="recycle">
    Create seamless looping videos where the start and end frames are the same.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Motion Control" icon="wind">
    Choose between high and low motion to control the amount of movement in your video.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Raw Mode" icon="sliders">
    For more precise motion control, use Raw Mode to have your prompt text have more influence on the outcome.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How to Use VidCraft

<Steps>
  <Step title="Input Prompt and Image">
    Enter a text prompt (optional) and upload an image to serve as the first frame of your video.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Options">
    Choose the options you wish to use, such as loop, raw, and the motion amount.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate Previews">
    Click "Generate". You will see a gradient glow and a "generation in progress" message. Once the generation is done, you will have a choice of 4 results.
    <Frame>
      <img src="https://pixiomedia.nyc3.digitaloceanspaces.com/docs/vidcraft1.png" alt="VidCraft Generation Progress" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upscale Your Video">
    Pick any one of the 4 results and click "Upscale".
    <Frame>
      <img src="https://pixiomedia.nyc3.digitaloceanspaces.com/docs/vidcraft2.png" alt="VidCraft Upscale" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Extend Your Video">
    You can then extend the video with either high motion or low motion.
    <Frame>
      <img src="https://pixiomedia.nyc3.digitaloceanspaces.com/docs/vidcraft3.png" alt="VidCraft Extend" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>
