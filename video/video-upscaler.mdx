## Best Practices for Video Upscaling

To achieve the best results with the Video Upscaler, consider these tips:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Choosing the Right Input Video" icon="film">
    - Use the highest quality source video available
    - Ensure the video is free from significant artifacts or distortions
    - Trim your video to the most important 5 seconds if it's longer
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Selecting the Optimal Resolution" icon="tv">
    - Choose 4K resolution for the best quality output
    - Consider your intended display medium when selecting resolution
    - Balance quality with file size based on your needs
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Using the Appropriate Model" icon="robot">
    - Use the standard model for real-life footage
    - Select the anime model for cartoon, anime, or cel-shaded content
    - Experiment with both models to see which gives better results for your specific video
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Post-Processing Considerations" icon="sliders">
    - Consider minor color grading after upscaling for enhanced visual appeal
    - Use video editing software to fine-tune contrast and sharpness if needed
    - Compare the upscaled video with the original to ensure desired improvements
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

If you encounter issues with the Video Upscaler, try these solutions:

1. **Upload Failures**: Ensure your video file is in a supported format and doesn't exceed the 5-second limit.
2. **Poor Upscaling Results**: Try using a higher quality source video or experiment with a different upscaling model.
3. **Processing Errors**: If the upscaling process fails, try re-uploading the video or using a different browser.
4. **Unexpected Artifacts**: For anime content, make sure you're using the anime-optimized model for best results.
